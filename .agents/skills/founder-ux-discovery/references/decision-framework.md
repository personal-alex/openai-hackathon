# UX Decision Framework

Compare concepts with the criteria that matter to the product: first-use clarity, cognitive load, discoverability, speed, trust, transparency, flexibility, scalability, AI fit, and complexity.

Do not select a winner mechanically. Prefer the direction that best fits the primary user’s real situation, core job to be done, emotional context, differentiator, and first-version constraints. Explain what it optimizes, what it sacrifices, what to borrow from other concepts, and what not to build yet.
