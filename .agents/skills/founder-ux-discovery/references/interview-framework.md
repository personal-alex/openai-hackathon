# Interview Framework

Use as a guide, not a questionnaire. Ask one question at a time and follow the most valuable thread.

## Retrieve before asking

Look in configured project memory for product vision, target users, workflows, prior UX decisions, rejected concepts, assumptions, open questions, and constraints. Do not repeat known answers; surface material conflicts.

## Explore

- **Product:** What is it, what problem does it solve, why does it matter, and what happens without it?
- **Users:** Who primarily uses it, who else is involved, who pays, decides, or is affected?
- **Context:** What triggers use; where is the user; what device, time, stress level, expertise, and frequency apply?
- **Outcome:** What does success mean; what builds trust; what causes abandonment?
- **Journey:** What precedes use; what decisions and steps follow; where do users get stuck; what happens after completion?
- **Data:** What is supplied, already known, required, optional, sensitive, or confidential?
- **AI:** What should AI understand, recommend, decide, explain, and leave in user control? What happens under uncertainty?
- **Constraints:** What technical, business, legal, regulatory, or first-version constraints apply?

Keep confirmed facts, founder assumptions, UX hypotheses, and open questions separate.
