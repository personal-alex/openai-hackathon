# Vision — Life Navigator

## The problem

When life changes, people need to know what comes next. Instead, they encounter disconnected institutions, dense language, forms, deadlines, and conflicting sources. The burden of translating a human event into an institutional workflow falls on the person at the moment they are most likely to need clarity.

## Our point of view

People experience **life events**, not organizational charts.

Life Navigator starts with a human statement such as “We're expecting our first child” or “I lost my job.” It understands the situation, asks for the context that changes the answer, and presents a clear path forward.

## The hackathon promise

The hackathon product is a Navigator:

- It understands a situation rather than merely answering an isolated question.
- It creates a personalized roadmap rather than returning a list of links.
- It explains the rationale behind every suggested step.
- It uses curated sources and clearly distinguishes guidance from certainty.

## What we are not building

For the hackathon, Life Navigator is not:

- a generic chat interface over web pages;
- a government portal replacement;
- an autonomous agent that submits forms or acts without user approval;
- a source of legal, medical, tax, or financial advice.

## Platform principle

Different life events are inputs to the same Life Event Engine. The MVP may demonstrate one event deeply, but the core concepts—event, context, question, knowledge, roadmap, and explanation—must be reusable across events and jurisdictions.

## Design north star

The interface should leave a person feeling calmer and more capable. It should always answer the practical question:

> What should I do next, and why?
