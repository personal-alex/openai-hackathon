# Founder UX Discovery — core outcome

## Confirmed founder insight

Life Navigator's core user outcome is **clarity and regained control**. After the first five minutes, a person should be able to say: “Now I know what I should do and what my next steps are.”

## Context

People facing major life events often do not know where to begin, flow with circumstances, and fear paperwork or missing important actions. General LLM chatbots can feel unreliable because usefulness depends on the model and exact wording of a prompt, which compounds loss of control.

## UX implication (hypothesis)

The experience must make a dependable, personally relevant route legible early. It cannot merely provide more answers; it must show what is known, what changes the route, and the next concrete action without demanding that the user become an expert.

## Open question

Which concrete moment or decision should be made easier first, before the route is fully understood?
