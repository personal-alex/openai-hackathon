# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Life Navigator serves people actively planning a significant life event. They need a clear view of the process they need to follow, without having to assemble it from a generic conversation or a dense information source.

## Product Purpose

The product turns a supported life event into a clear process roadmap. A successful first session gives a person a useful vision of the next steps that apply to the situation they choose to share.

## Positioning

For the hackathon MVP, Life Navigator is a guided, deterministic state machine that knows the approved steps for a supported life event; later stages may integrate with government and other systems to help fill or perform required steps.

## Brand Personality

Life Navigator is a virtual buddy. Its defining line is: “Life doesn’t come with instructions. Now it does.”

## Anti-references

Do not make the experience feel wizard-like or wiki-like. Avoid presenting heavy data on each screen; the flow should feel chat-like while remaining structured and purposeful.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the roadmap concrete: each interaction should clarify the next applicable step.
2. Let conversation carry the experience, while the deterministic roadmap remains the product artifact.
3. Reduce cognitive load by asking only what changes the plan.
4. Be a reassuring companion without becoming vague, overly conversational, or authoritative beyond the approved state.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The product targets a wide audience. No additional accessibility requirements were supplied beyond the existing inclusive web baseline.
